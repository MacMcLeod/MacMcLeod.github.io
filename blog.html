Have you ever heard of the purple dragon? As someone who is constantly on the lookout for electronic lookouts, who has covered up their cameras with electrical tape, and who still uses cash in the digital age, I was surprised to find out that I had no idea what the mascot for OPSEC was. Operations Security, or OPSEC, has it's roots in the history of war. As it turns out, the US OPSEC program's genesis was during the Vietnam war.

Chinese general Sun Tzu advised "Take advantage of the enemy's unpreparedness; travel by unexpected routes and strike him where he has taken no precautions." Early attempts by US forces to use surprise in the war were thwarted. The US Joint Chiefs of Staff authorized Operation Purple Dragon to find out why. The NSA was largely successful in determining that there were many points of weakness in the US strategy, allowing enemy combatants access to information that undercut any surprise efforts. The OPSEC strategies learned during this operation are still in use today.
